Mental Health Disorders

Borderline personality disorder

Symptoms: Some symptoms of borderline personality disorder include extreme mood swings, which result in emotional pain and unstable relationships with others. BPD can also make people act impulsively, experience feelings of dissociation, or self-harm.

Causes: There is no specific cause for BPD, however it can be influenced by family history, brain structure and function, as well as external factors such as childhood trauma.

Other: Borderline personality disorder is a condition that affects a person’s ability to regulate their emotions. This condition affects 4 million people nationwide and can be treated via psychotherapy with a licensed, trained mental health professional. Psychotherapy teaches people with borderline personality disorder to interact with others and express themselves effectively. Two types of psychotherapy include dialectical behavioral therapy (DBT) and cognitive behavioral therapy (CBT) that are targeted to improve upon certain symptoms of BPD.

Schizophrenia

Symptoms: Some symptoms of Schizophrenia include delusions, hallucinations, blunted emotions, apathy, social withdrawal, and in extreme cases suicidal thoughts.

Causes: Although the precise cause is not known, family history of schizophrenia, pregnancy complications, or taking mind-altering drugs as a teen/young adult can all increase the risk of schizophrenia.

Other: Schizophrenia is a mental condition that includes problems with thinking, behavior, and emotions. About 3.5 million people are diagnosed nationwide.

Alien Hand Syndrome

Symptoms: Some symptoms of Alien Hand Syndrome include involuntary groping, involuntary grasping, difficulty releasing objects, arm levitation, less purposeful movement, and psychological struggles.

Causes: Alien Hand Syndrome is developed after a stroke, tumor, or trauma and is often associated with cancer, neurodegenerative diseases, or brain aneurysms.

Other: It is a mental condition that allows one hand to act entirely on its own. This hand isn’t under the control of the person’s mind, but as if it has a mind of its own. Currently, there is no cure for this condition, but behavioral therapy have helped manage the conditions. This condition is extremely rare and hasn’t had many cases in the past.

Dissociative amnesia

Symptoms: The main symptom is memory loss that's more severe than normal forgetfulness that can't be explained by a medical condition. You can't recall information about yourself or events and people in your life, especially from a traumatic time.

Causes: The main cause of Dissociative amnesia is overwhelming stress. Overwhelming stress can be caused by traumatic events such as war, abuse, accidents, or disasters.

Other: Dissociative amnesia is a mental condition where the person can’t remember important facts from their life, either general memories or targeted specific areas. This condition is rare as it affects about 1% of men and 2.6% of women and is usually followed by conditions like war or natural disasters. In order to treat this mental condition, doctors usually send these patients to a mental health professional and they perform a clinical interview to understand the patient better. The treatment involves many therapies like psychotherapy and some medication.

Depersonalisation disorder

Symptoms: Some symptoms of depersonalization disorder include substance abuse, seizure disorders, and other brain disorders or diseases.

Causes: There is no exact known cause; however, environmental, biological and psychological factors do play a role.

Other: Depersonalization disorder is a disorder in which one feels disconnected from their body and their thoughts. It is described as looking at oneself from outside their body. This disorder can involve memory breakdown, consciousness awareness, and more. When any of these occur, symptoms of the depersonalization disorder can occur. Whilst in an episode of depersonalization, a person will feel like they are in a dream. It can last for a few minutes to possibly a few years (although that is more rare).

Dissociative identity disorder

Symptoms: Some symptoms of Dissociative Identity Disorder include memory loss, delusions, depression, anxiety, suicidal thoughts and behaviors, and a blurred sense of identity.

Causes: The cause of DID is usually a past traumatic event. This can include emotional, physical, or sexual abuse.

Other: Dissociative identity disorder (DID) is a disorder in which a person experiences multiple personalities. The various identities can control the person at different times. Ways to stay strong and cope with DID are by having a strong support system and taking therapy sessions which can reduce the shifts in personalities. Therapy focusing on managing and controlling behavioral changes and working through overcoming past trauma can help a person’s DID condition improve. In some scenarios, hypnotherapy can also help improve DID.
